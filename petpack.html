PetPack

This pet subscription box app aims to give pet owners a simple, fun, and efficient experience and incorporates a short quiz which helps to curate a personalized “PetPack” for their furry friend. Working with a group of four and utilizing the waterfall methodology, we developed the app prototype and our team's lead developer designed and developed an email template and landing page for our fictional brand.

1. Empathize & Define

The target audience we identified was dog and cat owners who were wanting to spoil their pets with high quality treats, fun toys, and accessories. We wanted to give our users a simple, fun, and efficient experience that would bring joy and get them excited about their monthly delivery. Our app's unique selling point was our short quiz, which would allow pet owners to have a more customizable, curated experience than what was offered by competitors. During this phase, Megan created a moodboard and a userflow chart to get a sense for the app's feel and functionality.

2. Ideate

I created our style guide including fonts, colours, and type scale and sketched several logo variations to present to the team. We decided on our preferred logo and I created a final version in Illustrator. I also created components in Figma including buttons, inputs, and icons. During this phase, Michael created an initial sketch for our landing page, and Michelle sketched wireframes for the app and solidified the questions we would have in our quiz.

3. Prototype

With Megan's userflow chart, Michelle's wireframes, and my style guide as our references, we began construction of our prototype using Figma. Megan created the necessary screens for our favourites pages, Michael created the screens for our user dashboard, Michelle created our quiz screens, and I created our login and sign-up screens, our product catalogue, and our individual product screens.

View Figma Prototype

4. Development

During the development phase, our lead developer, Michael, developed our responsive email template. Michael also designed and prototyped the desktop landing page for our fictional brand using Figma, which was intended to give users an introduction to the product's features, benefits, and functionality.

6. Reflection

This project was a deep dive into the waterfall methodology, and as a team we learned a lot about working in a linear fashion. However, we did not implement user testing of our prototype, landing page, or email template. Through feedback from user testing, we would be able to revise these deliverables in future iterations to enhance usability and user satisfaction.
