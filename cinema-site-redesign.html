Cinema Site Redesign

For this project, our team identified potential business problems in the website design of a cinema company and formulated three hypotheses. Through moderated user testing, we aimed to validate these hypotheses and design a user-centric website to resolve the identified issues.

Identify Business Problems & Establish Hypotheses

The business problems we identified were high bounce rates on the landing page and low conversion rates for online ticket sales.


Based on the business problems identified, we formulated three hypotheses:

“We believe that the large number of options in the main navigation would overwhelm users’ cognitive load and would hinder their ability to reach their goal efficiently.”

“We believe users who are interested in seeing a specific movie would be frustrated by the lack of a search feature, filters, and facets.”

“We believe that the cluttered ‘showtimes & tickets’ section may contribute to user abandonment of the website without selecting a movie.”

Candidate Screening

Before implementing our user tests, we screened 14 potential candidates to ensure that those selected would represent the user base and met the criteria required for the study. Of those 14 candidates, we selected a total of 4 participants to take part in our moderated user tests.

Testing

Moderated, in-person testing was conducted for our user studies, which were simultaneously recorded on zoom for further observations. Moderators followed task cards that we created to guide participants through a series of tasks and questions relating to the cinema company’s website. Once the user completed the tasks, we had each of them complete a SUS questionnaire about the website.

Observations

Following the moderated sessions, each user’s recording was viewed by two observers on our team who took notes using a Rainbow sheet, highlighting common observations. The primary purpose of our observations was to identify patterns in user behaviour and ultimately prove or disprove our hypotheses.


After analyzing our data, we found the results proved our hypotheses and clearly showed three key takeaways:

Users felt the Cinema’s website was difficult to navigate because of the unclear navigation labels and organization of content.

Users had difficulties finding specific movies and setting their location with the drop down menu.

Users felt the Cinema’s website was cluttered and overwhelming, making it challenging to view available movies.

Recommendations

Based on the results of our user testing and observations, we developed three recommendations:

Simplify the main navigation and use dropdown menus to indicate information hierarchy.

Include a search feature for both movies and locations to allow accessible and intuitive navigation.

De-clutter the ticket purchasing process to improve user experience.
